"sometimes I feel like you choose to be sad"

That's what my friend got told by the guy she likes - the guy that she is currently dating, the guy that doesn't seem to undertand the concept of depression.

Maybe it's because I come from a place where I dealt with those negative emotions, and my persepctive is obviously different - but i find that to be very narrow minded and dismissive and just plain, mean.

When she told me this, I explained how I viewed her and her depression.

To me, it's a state. It is a place where you are at. And its a place that makes you feel and think in a lower space than normal.

It's like looking at a horizon. The sky meets the sea meets the sand. And you should be looking at where the sun meets the sea, right? But you can't. You're struggling to acknowledge that it's there because you can only really see that the sand meets the water. Sometimes you look up and you see that horizon, and you think, Huh, that's pretty great".

But there's things happening around you, and sometimes inside you, that keep pushing you to rememeber that the sand meets the sea. And you can rememeber and experience the view of the horizon every now and then, but you're usually used to, and often still looking down at where the sand and the sea meet.

Okay, maybe I didn't say it exactly like that, but I'm sure you get the picture.

The point is. I know that she is trying her best, despite the things that are happening in and around her, to look up and see the horizon. To see the beautiful meeting of blue and orange, when everything is telling her it is only blue and the dull colour of sand.


And to me, that effort is a choice.
She is actively choosing to try when everything is telling her not to. She is trying so hard.

So is she really choosing to be sad?
