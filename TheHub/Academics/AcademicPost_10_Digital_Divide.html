The Impact of the Digital Divide.

The Digital Divide is greatest manifestation of Digital Inequality.

This form of inequality had not only become as prominent as traditional forms of inequality - which include: race, class, gender etc - but it has become intwined with the tradtional forms, as mentionsed by the authors of "Digital inequalites and why they matter". This divide plays a major role in several aspects of life. WIth the merging of the digital platforms and reality, the actions taken on either and the repercussions on both, the impact of the Digital Divide has allowed the world to indugle in a concept of technology dependency and insuffiency on several levels.

In the article by Carmen Steele, on the Digital Divide Council site, there is an exploration of several sectors that the Digital Divide has a large play in. Namely, education, the economy, social interaction, society as a whole, and culture. Steele explains how income and digital literacy are crucial contributors to the Digital Divide, but also that these do not reflect the only factors that contribute to the racial and ethnic imbalance of digital platforms today. In the article, "Digital inequalities and why they matter", the authors identified that race, ethinicity and gender - regardless of the country's high level of technological adoption - present equal disadvantage when it comes to the Digital Divide.The sectors that Steele elaborates on further display just how the disparities show themselves.

Although the internet serves as a rich accumlation of the world's knowledge, the lack of access to the internet itself already presents a fundamentally large gap - with no access to the internet, there is no access to it's knowledge. Without access to the knowledge of the how and what of the internet, there is no way to access it or further obtain knowlegde regarding it. Paired with this, the lack of technological literacy finds effect in the education sector, and the biggest victims of this, are countries with weak/new education systems - mainly in developing countries where the overall idea of technology is not as adept as that of the world that is comfortable with the medium. Another factor that is affected by this controlled knowledge, is the gender factor tat contributes to the divide, which is present in when it comes to both education and job opportunity.
