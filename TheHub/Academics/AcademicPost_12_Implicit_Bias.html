Reflecting on the Race Implicit Bias Test.

The Project Implicit is a NPO (non profit organisation) that allows for international collaboration between researcher who wish to explore implicit social cognition, or mor simply: "thoughts and feelings outside of conscious awareness and control". It allows for the edication of the public in terms of hidden biases and as a secondary objective, provides a virtual lab of data collection in the digital space.

I wil specifically be taking the Race Implicit Association Test and reflecting on the results. This test requires the the taker of the test tim distinguish the faces of people that are of European and African descent. The test has a general conclusion - that most Americans have an automatic preference for white over black. I believe that because of my unique South African context, that my results will most likely lean in the opposite to that of the general American public.

One issue I have already identified in the description of the test, is that although it encompasses the idea of race - it intially present the spectrum as two distinct binaries, ignoring the inclusion of other race, and thereby contributing to the exclusion of such. It is an issue I find very prevelant, especially from a backgorund that is neither black or white.

The Race Task when clicked on, furhter explains that the test will consist of sorting images and words into groups at a rapid pace. There are questions that help contextualise you as a test taker that pertain to your beliefs, attitudes, opinions and demographic.

The result of my test is, "No automatic preference between Black and White people".

I find this to be rather surprising. I have always felt that I have had a somewhat personal bias against the white community because of of the racial history of the the country I both live and was raised in, alongside several racially charged encounters where I was discriminated against by white people. I also felt that my circle of friends, which are predominately people of colour, would also establish a bias where I would automatically prefer Black over White. I also realise that this can be taken in the more negative where there is a lack of automatic association to either because of my own racial context that shows no current direct association to either.
I - since learning about the importance of intersectionality and justice, have been conciously aware of this bias. I believe this result shows that concious awareness plays a huge part in my views of equality, and despite my personal feelings of my bias, I respond in a neutral manner despite all the factors mentions above. I am proud of my result and will continue to pursue a state where my bias is neutral.

Based on results from Implicit Project Race IAT
